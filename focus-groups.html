Discovery Method

Focus Groups

Guided conversations with small groups to explore attitudes, ideas, and reactions to concepts. Good for generating initial insights.

Time Required

2-3 Weeks

Participants Needed

5-8 Users per group | 3-4 Groups

Difficulty Level

Advanced

Overview

Focus groups bring together small groups of participants in moderated discussions to explore attitudes, perceptions, and reactions. Group dynamics and conversation flow help reveal real insight into unique viewpoints, while having participants in both observers roles creates a safe space.

Usage

When to use

  • Exploring general attitudes
  • Gathering initial reactions
  • Generating new ideas
  • Understanding group dynamics
  • Testing concept appeal
  • Identifying shared pain points
  • Exploring cultural perspectives

When not to use

  • For usability testing
  • When collecting metrics
  • For sensitive topics
  • When consensus isn't helpful
  • For detailed personal experiences
  • When groupthink might skew results
  • For final decision making

Process

1

Planning

  • Define research objectives
  • Create discussion guide
  • Plan group composition
  • Choose facilities
  • Arrange recording
  • Set up incentives
  • Brief stakeholders
2

Recruitment

  • Define participant criteria
  • Screen participants
  • Form balanced groups
  • Send confirmations
  • Arrange logistics
  • Plan backup participants
3

Session Preparation

  • Set up room layout
  • Test recording equipment
  • Prepare materials
  • Brief observers
  • Arrange refreshments
  • Review discussion guide
4

Facilitation

  • Welcome participants
  • Set ground rules
  • Guide discussion
  • Manage group dynamics
  • Keep time
  • Document responses
  • Handle dominant voices
5

Analysis

  • Review recordings
  • Transcribe sessions
  • Code responses
  • Identify themes
  • Compare groups
  • Note outliers
  • Synthesize findings
6

Reporting

  • Document insights
  • Include key quotes
  • Highlight patterns
  • Share video clips
  • Present findings

Example Questions

  • "What are your initial thoughts about this concept?"
  • "How does this compare to what you currently use?"
  • "What would make this more appealing to you?"
  • "How might this fit into your daily routine?"
  • "What concerns would you have about this?"

Pairs well with

User Interviews

Why?

Follow up group discussion with one-on-one chats to dig deeper into personal experiences without group influence.

Surveys

Why?

Use surveys to validate focus group findings with a larger, more representative sample.
