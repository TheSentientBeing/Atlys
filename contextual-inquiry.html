Discovery Method

Contextual Inquiry

Watch users interact with your product in their real-world context to uncover authentic behaviors and needs.

Time Required

3-4 Weeks

Participants Needed

8-12 Users

Difficulty Level

Advanced

Overview

Contextual Inquiry combines observation and interviewing to understand how users interact with products in their natural environment. This method reveals genuine behavioral patterns, workarounds, and environmental factors that influence product usage, providing rich insights that might be missed in traditional lab settings.

Usage

When to use

Understanding complex workflows, discovering environmental influences, observing real user behaviors, learning about multi-device usage, exploring workplace dynamics, understanding team interactions, discovering integration points with other tools.

When not to use

When privacy or security concerns present, for testing specific feature implementations, when budget is extremely limited, for purely remote research, in highly controlled environments, when time is severely limited.

Process

1

Preparation & Planning

  • Define research goals
  • Create observation protocol
  • Design interview questions
  • Plan study logistics
  • Plan recording methods
  • Set up note taking templates
  • Brief team members
2

Participant Recruitment

  • Define criteria
  • Screen potential participants
  • Schedule site visits
  • Share pre-visit information
  • Brief participants
  • Confirm logistics
3

Field Observation

  • Introduction and setup
  • Observe natural workflow
  • Take detailed notes
  • Record key interactions
  • Ask contextual questions
  • Document environment
  • Capture artifacts
4

In-Situ Interviews

  • Probe observed behaviors
  • Ask for clarification
  • Explore pain points
  • Discuss workarounds
  • Understand context
  • Document team/things
5

Synthesis & Analysis

  • Organize field notes
  • Identify patterns
  • Map user flows
  • List environmental factors
  • Document pain points
  • Create user journey maps
  • Develop recommendations

Example Questions

  • "Can you walk me through how you typically complete this task?"
  • "What tools or resources do you use alongside our product?"
  • "How does your physical workspace affect how you work?"
  • "What steps in your environment influence your process?"

Pairs well with

Journey Mapping

Why?

Use journey mapping to visualize and communicate the insights you collect, help understand behaviors observed during contextual inquiry sessions.

User Interviews

Why?

Combines traditional contextual observations with in-depth interviews to better understand user motivations and mental models.
