Discovery Method

Ethnographic Research

Immerse yourself in users' daily lives to deeply understand their behaviors and context.

Time Required

4-12 Weeks

Participants Needed

5-10 Users/Communities

Difficulty Level

Advanced

Overview

Ethnographic research involves immersing researchers in users' environments to understand their behaviors, cultural context, and social dynamics. This method provides rich, nuanced insights into how products fit into broader life patterns and cultural frameworks.

Usage

When to use

  • Understanding cultural influences
  • Exploring complex social dynamics
  • Mapping contextual factors
  • Understanding community practices
  • Investigating sensitive topics
  • Mapping social ecosystems
  • Identifying hidden connections

When not to use

  • When quick insights are needed
  • For testing specific features
  • When deep immersion is restricted
  • For strictly technical evaluations
  • When privacy concerns prevent observation
  • For short-term product decisions

Process

1

Research Design & Planning

  • Define research objectives
  • Select community
  • Design methodology
  • Address ethics
2

Community Entry

  • Gain access
  • Build relationships
  • Set expectations
  • Establish trust
3

Field Immersion

  • Participate in daily life
  • Observe and document
  • Record sensory impressions
  • Collect artefacts
4

Iterative Analysis

  • Review field notes
  • Identify patterns
  • Generate hypotheses
  • Adjust approach
5

Synthesis

  • Build theoretical framework
  • Discuss insights
  • Share initial findings
  • Develop recommendations
6

Validation & Exit

  • Verify interpretations
  • Share preliminary findings
  • Plan graceful exit
  • Maintain relationships

Example Questions

  • "How do cultural norms influence product usage?"
  • "What social dynamics affect decision-making?"
  • "How do community practices shape behaviors?"
  • "What parallel needs emerge from daily routines?"

Pairs well with

Journey Mapping

Why?

Use journey mapping to visualize and communicate the complex social and cultural patterns observed during ethnographic studies.

Contextual Inquiry

Why?

Complement broad ethnographic insights with focused observations of specific tasks and interactions.
